Glossary of love relationship terms

glossary of terms

A glossary of terms relating to love and relationships

Ghosting

Ghosting is a modern term for the act of abruptly ceasing all communication in the midst of what is a progressively good relationship.

Often this sees the 'ghoster' ignoring their love partners texts, phone calls, letters and all other forms of communication.

Being on the receiving end of a 'ghoster' is damaging and bewildering, it causes confusion and it is a form of emotional cruelty as a result of inflicting feelings of abandonment on the recipient.

Sometimes a 'ghoster' will use this tactic as a means to end a relationship, because they fear having a confrontational dialogue with their partner should they tell them to their face that they want to end the relationship, suffice to say, they view resorting to ghosting as the way to end things, or at the very least, to obtain space from their partner for a while.

Haunting

Often the act of 'haunting' follows on from 'ghosting'. This is where the ghoster, suddenly re-establishes contact after a period of 'ghosting'. The proverbial 'rolling back of the bad penny'! Often the act of 'haunting' is without any apparent explanation, it will come without any prior warning. It is often indirect - via perhaps social media as opposed to something more direct such as a phone call or a direct message sent to their former partner's phone. This fuels the theory that resorting to ghosting is a kind of 'cowards way of suspending or ending a love relationship'.

Orbiting

In essence, 'orbiting' is the new 'ghosting'. In this case a person will resort to monitoring you via social media, but without making direct contact with you themselves. It is a form of cyberstalking. Often a curious natured person will orbit you, they won't want to block you or unfollow you but they will want to observe you and what you post from a safe distance! 'Nowt so queer as folk' as they say up North!

Zombieing

The strange act of 'Zombieing' is where someone will re-appear in your life after having left your life months or even years earlier, a bit like 'coming back from the dead'! This often comes with the Zombieing person trying to trick you into believing that they are a completely changed person to that they once were. They will try to assure you that they didn't mean to ghost you but please don't fall for this old trick, it is more often the case that 'Leopards never change their spots'!

Submarining

This is rather similar to haunting, except it isn't as subtle in that they will just completely reappear just as a submarine does when it emerges up from the deep of the sea, completely visible to you. This will come without even any form of acknowledgment or apology for them having ghosted you. Sadly when this occurs it is not with any intent to offer any real connection or for that matter, a lasting one. Don't be fooled.

Breadcrumbing

Breadcrumbers are game players, they leave a trail of breadcrumbs in the form of text, messages, social media comments, gestures all designed to make you feel that they have a genuine interest in you when the fact is they do not. This term stems from the fairy tale story of 'Hansel and Gretel', the tactic is to indulge you in the fantasy that they are interested in you but without any real satisfaction or desired outcome. It is cruel and seldom progresses into anything of a full-blown or committed relationship, effectively stringing you along on a hiding to nothing, as-it-were. This is common on dating websites.

Situationship

This is another negative experience. It is where two people come together into a relationship out of perhaps 'convenience' and in the absence of genuine interest. It's a bit like a 'marriage of convenience' where two people stay together for anything other than a romantic interest or bond. Or where a married couple might 'stay together for the sake of the children' when they have no real bond left between them. It's a sterile relationship of sorts. It is common in situations where people fall for their neighbour because it saves the commute to one further afield! It's 'convenient'. Of course there is an end date which is sadly inevitable. People will so easily move on from such a relationship 'situation'!

Benching

Benching more often than not, involves some 'breadcrumbing'. It occurs when someone is not ready for a relationship but they are interested in you, so they sit on the benches as one would when watching a sporting event, eager to be a part of the game but not yet ready to play it full swing, so effectively they observe you, stalk you, watch you, assess you. Sadly those engaging in such activity end up dismayed when the person they are benching finds a partner and they end up being gazumped as is often the case!

The slow fade

Unlike with ghosting which sees sudden or abrupt end to a love relationship, the 'slow fade' is essentially the death of a relationship via a thousand cuts. The end will happen over a longer period, with one person pulling away from the other more slowly. This means a painful slow recession into nothingness and it will waste time. A sudden abrupt end or a break up after a discussion that things are not working out which leads to an ending, is often viewed as a kinder way to part, but this is a matter of opinion of course!

Accountability

This is a term for the act of maturely taking ownership of one's actions, cheerfully accepting their consequences and without blame, justification or resentment. Accountability stems from honesty and responsibility and it is not the same as 'taking the blame', it is positive accountability.

Boundaries

One might refer to boundaries as 'limits' and we all have them! The problem often arises when there are differences between one person and another, so these vary from one relationship to another, but often they are set without the cooperation from the other partner and not open to negotiation. They determine the individuals level of tolerance with regard to particular or specific aspects of the relationship.

There is however, a difference between a boundary and a threat. A threat, for example is where one will state “if you ever hit me, I will end the relationship”. If you don't mean what you say however, then the statement is a threat and not a boundary. Boundaries are a form of control that facilitate a position of safety to be ourselves.

Commitment

Commitment in the positive sense, is where both parties can agree on a certain level of commitment to each other.

Commitment can be viewed however, in different ways. In the case of one partner preferring a more 'open relationship' whereby there is the absence of exclusivity, this can be in conflict with a partner who seeks exclusivity and a more one on one arrangement, so it is important to ascertain each others' definition of commitment, from the onset, it will save a considerable amount of heartbreak further down the line.

Distancing

This is the act of moving away just enough both physically and emotionally, in order to gain a perceived safe distance from influence or obligation, but at the same time, remaining close enough to monitor and track the other person.

Whilst this differs from separation and abandonment, it often provokes the fear of being abandoned by the other. Efforts to examine why distance is desired, would resolve the potential for a parting.

Dominance

Often this is a term associated with 'the control freak' where one person's desires, wishes or goals have to fit in with that determined by the other. 'When in Rome do as the Roman's do' springs to mind here! Do as I say and like it! The proverbial 'Alpha Male' is often accredited with dominance, usually over a servile female who is obliging and will happily play a more submissive or subservient role.

Empathy

Empathy as with 100% trust are the key elements that form a long term and enduring love relationship.

In the absence of empathy and trust a true love bond cannot exist.

Empathy is present where each partner is fully able to understand and comprehend the emotions and desires of the other. It is where we can identify with those feelings and where we do not judge or dispute these feelings or emotions. Empathy facilitates a knowningness of how you would feel if you were in their shoes. We also empathize by feeling what our partner is feeling, so if our partner is hurt, we will be too, it does not however, come with full interpersonal powers of empathy and as such often comes without reasoning alone.

Gaslighting

Gaslighting is where one person attempts to undermine the sanity of the other, perhaps making them feel like they were crazy. This is to gain control and manipulate the victim.

Gaslighting derived its name from the 1944 movie called “Gaslight”, it starred Charles Boyer and Ingrid Bergman. Boyer 'gaslights' his new wife played by Bergman, by turning the gas on and off from the attic in their home, when Bergman reports these events to Boyer he disputes the reality of this in order to make her believe she is crazy. Sometimes there is a sinister purpose to Gaslighting and it can be to conceal an affair as in the move “Gaslight” but it isn't always the case. Gaslighting is the act of undermining in order to gain control and to success in manipulating the other person into a submissive position. It is cruel. It often leads to the victim being plunged into a sort of domestic Stockholm Syndrome, where the victim becomes trapped in the abusive relationship, seemingly without an escape route.

Reparation

This is the act of gifting goods or time to the injured party in an attempt to make them feel whole again. This can be via both direct and indirect means.

Rumination

Attempts to undo the past in order to change the situation or status quo constitute rumination, even if only by one merely thinking it. A change in one's perception can often make one feel a lot better about the past, though perceptual changes seldom occur from just thinking alone, if anything this can be a perpetual cycle which leads nowhere, in reality.

Taking the blame

The act of directing the bitter energies and resentments back toward oneself, in order to gain approval of the other partner, is not a stable and lasting solution to discord, this is because blame all too quickly leads back into resentment.

Trolling

This is sadly a common phenomenon, it is a destructive one. It is where one person takes ti inviting the other to state a preference or opinion and then delivering a response in the form of a counter opinion which is both offensive and a personal attack on the other person. It is a means of setting up what is known as a 'projective identification' - the act of manipulating another person's position or attitude via interpersonal accusation founded on the exploitation of the sensitivities of the other person. This leads to the injured party losing composure whilst protesting about the other person's actions. The injurer then claims that the actions of the injured party is justified. Projective identification is often usually effective because it is successful in stimulating fear, guilt, shame and anxiety in the victim benefiting the 'projecting person'.

White Projection

This is a term associated with the incidence of a person having the inability to see what others are plainly doing (as in denial). It is particularly common in those who see others doing what they would never do themselves. This can lead to the person becoming blinded to the fact that they are being taken advantage of by the other person. Self control or honesty are not strong enough to product 'white projection' they tend to arise from 'reaction formations'.
