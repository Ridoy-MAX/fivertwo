Fun facts

I met my soul mate by accident and a wonderful accident that was too! He appeared to be rather conservative in his appearance when he first walked toward me; I was used to the more unconventional sort of guy. I had dressed down and he had dressed up – I felt like a real peasant, but it didn’t seem to matter, because our connection we felt was just phenomenal. I knew from that moment on we were destined to be – we have remained on cloud nine ever since! I believe that this is how it should be for true soul mates.

I remember telling people at school that I wanted to become a Vet as I adored being around pets and animals. I had always enjoyed growing up in a household full of different pets, from a pet lamb and goats to rabbits and a cat and dog. I never imagined coming into my present vocation back then. But I am pleased to say that I have achieved the balance by adopting rescue animals and enjoying the buzz from enriching the lives of both human and animals. I do love to spoil people! Pets teach you about the value of unconditional love as does being with a soulmate partner.

I tried my hand at horse riding when I was 12 years old, pleading with the stable owners to allow me the pleasure of riding a lovely white and grey stallion called Misty. They caved in and I got to ride her, but I think she knew I was a novice and was perhaps a little nervous at having me on her back. She reared up onto her hind legs and the stable owner and my mother who were watching, shouted me to just jump off. I stayed on her and pulled on her reins until eventually she calmed down and put all of her legs back on the ground. I patted her on the side of her neck and carefully climbed off much to everyone’s amazement. The stable owner asked me where I learned to control a horse like that, as I was clearly a natural. The fact was, it was my first time on horseback. My saving grace was keeping calm in what could have been a very injurious crisis. A cool head sure pays off!

My passion is cooking, but sometimes the best accidents in the kitchen are the tastiest!

My daughter adored chocolate cake and pleaded with me to bake a large Chocolate cake, I had so much work to be getting on with and yet I didn’t want to deny her a favourite treat. I grabbed some cake mix and baked the cake, in between trying to do lots of other jobs and whilst I was at it I also baked some Chocolate muffins. I set them to cool on a rack in the kitchen. Later on I went back into the kitchen to decorate and fill the cakes only to see that the large chocolate cake had sunk in the middle and looked rather unappetising. I was going to throw it into the bin and bake another one, but my daughter begged me not to as she didn’t want to wait any longer for her treat and she said she wanted to try that one as opposed to the more perfect muffins. I cut the large cake and to our surprise the centre was all lovely and gooey with runny chocolate. It was delicious.

It just goes to show that appearances can be deceptive. The most unappetizing and plain looking cake can turn out to be the moist yummy! If only this was applied to how people view the aesthetically challenged souls who adorn our world, who can be some of the most beautiful people you could ever wish to meet.
