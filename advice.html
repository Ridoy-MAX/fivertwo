Advice on the psycho-dynamics of love relationships

Advice

Psycho-dynamics in love relationships need to be better understood

What is meant by psycho-dynamics

The path of true love is not always a smooth one as many people know from the challenges they experience in their love relationships.

The psycho-dynamics of love relationships is about understanding and analyzing the complexity of the way in which two people relate to each other.

Often it's about the psychology of perception, which determines the differences of view point and opinion and how we interpret the signals and comprehend the natural differences between the way you might see things compared to the way your love partner sees things.

One of you may need more space and 'me time' than the other, requests for 'more space' or a temporary break from each other can often be misinterpreted as the dreaded precursor to a complete break or lead to the assumption that there is 'someone else' around and you are being sidelined or dumped. Insecurities soon creep in and inevitably lead to discord, altercations and even heated rows; when the reality could have been that your partner just needed some temporary space to consider how they might want to progress the relationship and at what speed, particularly in new relationships -post the initial romantic 'honeymoon period' which comes with the beginning of most new relationships.

In the initial 'honeymoon period' many couples find themselves engaging in avid texting, sexting, voice and video calling on various chat app s, eager to meet and socialize together, wine and dine, the hearts and flowers routine is wonderful but sadly it can be as exhausting as it can a pleasure, and for one of other partner it can soon feel like its all a bit too full on and that things are moving a tad to fast, so they find the need to slam on the brakes and request a bit of space to try to balance off their new-found love interest with their former lifestyle, which involved seeing more of their own friends, pursuing hobbies, working late, spending time with family and just about everything else. Often a new love interest sidelines the former lifestyle interests and the imbalance can cause conflict with the retention of ones own individuality and lifestyle. So a bit of space is about re-grouping and balancing and often nothing more - but it is all too easy for the more insecure of us, to misinterpret this as a brush off, being replaced, being abused, used, demoted etc.

Sadly insecurities in such situations can often lead to significant damage being done to the former lovely rapport you once had when the feelings of being sidelined or replaced amidst a misinterpretation of the situation, occurs, it is often the case that the insecure will bombard their partner with endless messages and texts, demanding clarification as to what is going on, why the need for space, is there someone else around, is there a future for you with your loved one. Bombarding someone with endless messages after they have asked for some space just to re-group constitutes harassment, it can even lead to stalking activities and this is when any hope of a future with a loved one soon evaporates and can lead to the recipient feeling forced to block any incoming messages or phone calls - this can often be a point of no return and this in turn is determined by how forgiving the recipient is. We are all individuals and as a result, we will all react differently to that of others.

The fact that we all react differently to challenges in love relationships, is what determines their psycho-dynamics and how things are viewed, handled, managed and resolved. What suits the Goose doesn't always suit the gander! It would be foolish to expect your partner to take on your viewpoint and needs all the time and vice verse! So its about give and take, making allowances for the differences in need and viewpoint. Compromising where possible and in as fair and amicable way as is possible. If you want to stay together and you are struggling with the psycho-dynamics of your love relationship then please call us and let us help to prevent a break up.

The importance of trust

Often our insecurities step from past hurt, past failed relationships in what ever form they may have taken, being cheated on in the past, deceived and lied to, can often for the basis for mistrust. This in turn can lead to the wrong conclusions being reached when for instance your love partner requests some space from you or some 'me time', a temporary break. If you are unable to trust your love partner 100% then you really don't have a relationship worthwhile pursuing. The best thing would then to be counseling to resolve your feelings of insecurities in order to save a break up with your loved one or to address what it is about their behaviour perhaps, which leads you to have no trust in them, and failing that you are faced with simply letting go and moving on to someone new, and someone who you can trust 100% and who will trust you 100%, true soul mate relationships are ones where there is 100% trust from both sides. Are you struggling with a 'trust' problem? If so then we are here to help you, and we will! All is not lost if you get the right help as soon as possible.

Making comparisons & rebound relationships

We have all experienced that 'puppy love' from our teenage years, that 'first love' experience which always makes a massive impact on us. But as with all past love experiences - every love relationship differs, because we are all individuals and so it is often the case that people make the grave mistake of comparing a present partner to a past one and more often than not one which had the greatest impact on us, be it that first love partner or one since.

Many people try to get over a broken relationship by quickly moving onto a new one, they want to rebuild their damaged self-esteem which stems from perhaps being rejected or dumped by the last partner. Rebound relationships, however, are often doomed to ultimate failure, because you will not have given yourself time to mend and regain your emotional balance.

The tendency to 'over-demanding'

The wonderful feelings of a new-found love interest often see us getting a tad carried away in the whirlwind effect of that lovely romance. Of course, we would love it to continue on in the same avid fashion where you 'can't get enough of one another', texting and messaging is full on, you are love struck and love sick and yes, love is a sickness! An addiction! But it is natural for this lovely period of new-found romance to progress on into a more leveled and balanced one as we start to settle into our new relationship.

This natural cooling can unearth the insecurities of many and the result is that they slip into 'over-demanding' mode, much always wanting more and more. When this happens damage begins and especially when your partner is feeling rather stifled and besieged as indeed they soon will. Communication & the psychology of perception

Good communication in all relationships, including love relationships, is vital to their endurance.

When communication breaks down, for whatever reason, this can be the start of either an impasse situation where one or other party perhaps due to an obstinate nature, refuses to communicate at all, or if they do, it is on their terms only.

Disagreements and rows, are often due to one misunderstanding the other, misinterpreting the signals or what is communicated verbally, be it person to person in speech or via text or some other form of electronic communication.

One person's perception of a situation may differ from that of another, this can lead to misinterpretation. It's often about the 'psychology of perception'.

Many people suspect that if their partner asks for some space or a break, that it must be that they are having an affair with a 3rd party, when this might not be the case. Insecurities stemming from being cheated on in the past, will consequently fuel such suspicion but that doesn't mean that a wrong conclusion is justifiable. It isn't nice to be accused of infidelity when you are loyal and trustworthy by nature and disinclined to cheat.

Some people are not very good at communication, perhaps they are of a more 'reserved' nature and like periods of time alone in their own space, or they are people who find full on relationships too stifling. Sadly such people can find themselves being accused of secrecy, cheating and are tarred with the same brush as it were, as a person who is inclined to cheat.

It is vital that communication is worked on, nurtured, maintained and balanced, so that a love relationship can endure long term.
